# Chapter 4: Infection Prevention and Control

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:
- Explain the chain of infection and how to break it
- Perform proper handwashing technique
- Use personal protective equipment correctly
- Apply standard precautions in all care situations
- Prevent cross-contamination
- Respond appropriately to exposure incidents

---

## 4.1 Understanding Infection

An **infection** occurs when harmful microorganisms (germs, bacteria, viruses, fungi) invade the body and multiply. Infections can be serious or even life-threatening, especially in vulnerable populations like elderly residents.

### Why Infection Prevention Matters:
- Residents in care facilities are often elderly, weakened, or ill
- Healthcare-associated infections extend hospital stays and increase costs
- Proper technique protects residents, staff, and visitors
- Breaking the infection chain prevents disease transmission

---

## 4.2 The Chain of Infection

For an infection to occur, six elements must be present. Breaking any link stops transmission.

```
CHAIN OF INFECTION
│
├─ 1. INFECTIOUS AGENT (germ)
├─ 2. RESERVOIR (where it lives)
├─ 3. PORTAL OF EXIT (how it leaves)
├─ 4. MODE OF TRANSMISSION (how it travels)
├─ 5. PORTAL OF ENTRY (how it gets in)
└─ 6. SUSCEPTIBLE HOST (person it infects)
```

### Breaking the Chain:

**1. Remove the Agent:**
- Clean and disinfect surfaces
- Use proper cleaning products
- Sterilize equipment

**2. Eliminate the Reservoir:**
- Change soiled dressings
- Empty and clean bedpans
- Change contaminated linens
- Dispose of waste properly

**3. Block the Exit:**
- Cover nose/mouth when coughing
- Use tissues and dispose properly
- Wear face masks when indicated

**4. Interrupt Transmission:**
- Hand hygiene (most important!)
- Use clean/sterile technique
- Use personal protective equipment
- Maintain clean environment
- Proper waste disposal

**5. Prevent Entry:**
- Cover open wounds
- Keep skin intact
- Avoid touching face/eyes
- Don't share eating utensils

**6. Protect the Host:**
- Promote good nutrition
- Support adequate rest
- Encourage immunizations
- Maintain healthy behaviors

## 4.3 Handwashing - Your Most Important Tool

**Proper handwashing is the single most effective way to prevent infection.**

### When to Wash Hands:
- Before and after each resident
- Before eating or handling food
- After using the bathroom
- Before putting on gloves
- After removing gloves
- After touching contaminated items
- After coughing or sneezing
- Anytime they look soiled

### Proper Handwashing Technique (6-step procedure):

**Step 1: Wet**
- Use warm running water
- Wet hands and wrists

**Step 2: Apply Soap**
- Use enough soap to cover all surfaces
- Regular soap is fine (antibacterial not necessary)

**Step 3: Lather**
- Lather all surfaces including:
  - Palms and backs of hands
  - Between all fingers
  - Under and around nails
  - Wrists

**Step 4: Rub**
- Rub for at least 20 seconds
- Vigorous rubbing removes more germs
- Remember: count to 20 or hum "Happy Birthday" twice

**Step 5: Rinse**
- Rinse under running water
- Point fingertips downward
- Allow water to flow from wrists to fingertips
- This prevents clean water from running back onto arms

**Step 6: Dry**
- Dry with clean paper towel
- Use paper towel to turn off faucet
- This prevents recontamination

### What NOT to Do:
- Don't touch the inside of the sink after washing
- Don't let hands touch the faucet after washing
- Don't air dry without a towel
- Don't use communal cloth towels
- Don't skip between fingers or under nails
- Don't wash for less than 20 seconds

## 4.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

PPE creates a barrier between you and potentially infectious material.

### Types of PPE:

**Gloves:**
- Use when contact with blood or body fluids is possible
- Change between residents
- Remove gloves before touching face or clean items
- Always wash hands after glove removal

**Gown:**
- Wear when splashing is possible
- Cover from shoulders to knees and front/back
- Remove carefully to avoid contamination
- Don't reuse; use fresh gown for each resident

**Face Mask:**
- Wear if resident has respiratory infection
- Covers nose and mouth completely
- Don't reuse masks
- Replace if damp or damaged

**Eye Protection:**
- Wear if splashing to face/eyes is possible
- Covers both eyes
- Don't touch face while wearing

**Respiratory Protection (N95):**
- Special training required
- Used for airborne diseases (TB, measles)
- Must be fitted properly

### Proper PPE Removal:
1. Remove gloves first (turn inside out as you remove)
2. Remove face mask by straps (not the front)
3. Remove gown carefully
4. Wash hands immediately

## 4.5 Standard Precautions

**Standard precautions** are infection prevention practices used with ALL residents, regardless of diagnosis.

### Key Principles:
- Treat all blood and body fluids as potentially infectious
- Don't rely on diagnosis to determine precautions needed
- Assume anyone could have an infection

### Standard Precautions Include:
- Hand hygiene
- PPE use
- Respiratory etiquette
- Safe needle practices
- Proper waste disposal
- Environmental cleaning
- Linen handling procedures

### Additional Precautions for Specific Infections:
- **Contact Precautions:** Special gowning/gloves if direct contact
- **Droplet Precautions:** Mask if within 3 feet (respiratory infections)
- **Airborne Precautions:** Special masks for TB, measles, etc.

---

## 4.6 Handling Contaminated Items

### Soiled Linens:
- Hold away from your body and uniform
- Don't shake linens (spreads germs)
- Place in designated contaminated linen bag
- Don't overstuff bags
- Wash hands after

### Sharps (Needles, Broken Glass):
- Never recap needles
- Place in designated sharps container
- Report broken glass to supervisor
- Never pick up sharps with bare hands

### Waste and Biohazard Materials:
- Double-bag if leaking or soaked
- Follow facility waste disposal procedures
- Don't touch your face while handling waste
- Wash hands thoroughly

### Specimen Collection:
- Use sterile containers as instructed
- Label with resident identification
- Keep containers closed during transport
- Wear gloves when collecting

---

## 4.7 Exposure Incidents

An **exposure incident** occurs when you have potential contact with blood or body fluids (needle stick, splash in eyes, cut skin).

### If Exposure Occurs:

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Stop the activity**
2. **Wash the area** thoroughly with soap and water
3. **For eyes/mouth:** Rinse with water or saline
4. **Report to your supervisor immediately**
5. **Do not panic**

### Next Steps:
- Document the incident
- Identify the resident (if possible)
- Receive medical evaluation
- Consider testing based on circumstances
- Follow facility exposure protocol
- Report to OSHA if required

### Your Rights:
- Paid medical evaluation
- Free testing if offered
- No discrimination for reporting
- Confidentiality of results

---

## 4.8 Special Situations

### Multi-Drug Resistant Infections (MRSA, VRE, C. difficile):
- Use contact precautions (gown and gloves)
- Keep equipment in the room
- Don't share equipment with other residents
- Strict hand hygiene is critical
- May require private room or cohorting

### Bloodborne Pathogens (HIV, Hepatitis B/C):
- Standard precautions prevent transmission
- Needles are not reused
- Sharps are in designated containers
- Spills are cleaned with bleach solution (1:10 ratio)

### Respiratory Infections (COVID-19, Influenza, TB):
- May require masking
- Droplet or airborne precautions possible
- Enhance hand hygiene
- Clean high-touch surfaces frequently

---

## 4.9 Environmental Cleaning

### Daily Cleaning:
- Clean high-touch surfaces (door handles, remotes, phones)
- Use approved cleaning agents
- Disinfect bathrooms thoroughly
- Empty trash daily
- Change water in residents' pitchers

### Spill Cleanup:
- Wear gloves
- For body fluids: use bleach solution (1 part bleach to 10 parts water)
- Let sit for at least 10 minutes
- Wipe clean with paper towels
- Dispose of materials in biohazard if needed
- Wash hands

### Terminal Cleaning:
- Deep clean when resident leaves
- Clean under furniture
- Clean and disinfect all surfaces
- Replace soiled mattresses
- Clean closets and storage areas

---

## Key Takeaways

✓ Proper handwashing is your single best infection prevention tool  
✓ Use standard precautions with ALL residents  
✓ Always use appropriate PPE for the situation  
✓ Break the chain of infection at every possible link  
✓ Handle contaminated items carefully and separately  
✓ Report exposure incidents immediately  
✓ Maintain a clean, disinfected environment  
✓ Ask if unsure—infection prevention is everyone's responsibility
