## STUDY STRATEGIES FOR SUCCESS

### Effective Study Methods:

**1. Active Recall:**
- Don't just re-read chapters
- Close the book and test yourself
- Use flashcards regularly
- Explain concepts out loud

**2. Spaced Repetition:**
- Review Chapter 1 repeatedly over time
- Don't cram all chapters at once
- Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 30 review schedule
- Flashcards daily for 2-3 weeks before exam

**3. Interleaving:**
- Mix chapters, don't study only one topic
- Combine different difficulty levels
- Alternate between reading and quizzing
- Study related but different concepts together

**4. Elaboration:**
- Explain WHY answers are correct
- Connect concepts to real situations
- Teach the material to others
- Create your own examples

**5. Retrieval Practice:**
- Take practice quizzes repeatedly
- Retake chapters where you scored below 80%
- Simulate exam conditions with timed tests
- Focus on previously missed questions

### Study Session Structure (25-30 minutes):
1. **Review (5 min):** Quick flashcard review
2. **Learn (15 min):** Read new chapter section
3. **Practice (8 min):** Answer 2-3 practice questions
4. **Reflect (2 min):** Note concepts to review later

### Study Environment:
- Quiet space with minimal distractions
- Comfortable but not too relaxed seating
- Adequate lighting to reduce eye strain
- Phone on silent or away
- Have water and take periodic breaks
- Study when you're most alert (morning for many)

## TEST-TAKING STRATEGIES

### Before the Exam:
1. Get 7-9 hours of sleep the night before
2. Eat a healthy breakfast
3. Arrive 15 minutes early
4. Use the bathroom before starting
5. Take deep breaths to calm anxiety

### During the Exam:
1. **Read carefully** - Don't rush through questions
2. **Eliminate obvious wrong answers** - Increases odds
3. **Look for clues in questions** - Questions often have hints
4. **Flag and return** - Skip hard questions, return later
5. **Manage time** - Don't spend over 2 min per question
6. **Trust your preparation** - Go with your first instinct usually

### Question Types:
- **Knowledge questions:** Direct recall (use flashcards)
- **Application questions:** Apply knowledge to scenarios (use case studies)
- **Analysis questions:** Determine best action (think through consequences)
- **Prioritization questions:** Pick most important action (think safety first)

### Common Answer Patterns:
- Longest answer is often correct (but not always)
- "All of the above" is frequently wrong
- "Never" and "always" are usually wrong
- Safety is almost always the priority

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How long should I study?**
A: Typically 4-6 weeks, 4-5 hours per week. Adjust based on your learning pace.

**Q: What if I fail the first time?**
A: Most people pass with proper preparation. Review weak areas and try again within a few weeks.

**Q: Do I need to memorize everything?**
A: No—focus on understanding WHY things are done correctly. Comprehension leads to better retention.

**Q: Should I study alone or with others?**
A: Both are valuable. Mix solitary study with group review sessions.

**Q: How much will the exam focus on dementia care?**
A: About 8-10% of the exam. Important but not majority of content.

**Q: What are the most commonly missed topics?**
A: Infection control, vital signs accuracy, and prioritization of safety.

**Q: Is there a time limit on the exam?**
A: Yes, typically 2-3 hours. Work steadily but don't rush.

**Q: Can I retake the exam if I don't pass?**
A: Yes, typically within 3-6 months. Requirements vary by testing company.
